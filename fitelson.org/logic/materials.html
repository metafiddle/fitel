Readings

Part I: Propositional (a.k.a., Sentential/Boolean) Logic

  • Unit #1: Basic Logical Vocabulary and Concepts (Propositions, Arguments, Validity, Soundness, etc.)
    • Chapter 1 of Forbes's Modern Logic
      • Chapter 1 of Hardegree's Symbolic Logic
      • Logical Constants, by John MacFarlane, Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
  • Unit #2: Symbolizations in Propositional (a.k.a., Sentential/Boolean) Logic
    • Chapter 2 of Forbes's Modern Logic
      • Chapter 4 of Hardegree's Symbolic Logic
  • Unit #3: Semantics for Propositional (a.k.a., Sentential/Boolean) Logic
    • Chapter 3 of Forbes's Modern Logic
      • Chapter 2 of Hardegree's Symbolic Logic
      • Chapter 3 of Hardegree's Symbolic Logic

Part II: Probability Calculus and (Sentential) Inductive Logic

  • Unit #4: Probability Calculus as a Generalization of Deductive (Sentential) Logic
    • Probability and Inductive Logic (Chapter 2 of Brian Skyrms's Choice and Chance)
    • Probability and Logic (Sections 2.1–2.3 of Ernest Adams's A Primer of Probability Logic)
    • Conditional Probabilities and Conditionalization (Sections 4.1& 4.2 of Ernest Adams's A Primer of Probability Logic)
      • "Inductive Logic", my entry in Philosophy of Science: An Encyclopedia, (J. Pfeifer and S. Sarkar, eds.)
      • Interpretations of Probability, by Alan Hájek, Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
